As your Program Administrator, I have designed this framework to demystify the institutional barriers often faced by first-generation students. Funding your degree is a strategic operation.

01 The Student Lifecycle Journey

Incoming Freshmen

Navigating the Portal

Your first semester is high-risk. You must set up your EUID and Duo MFA immediately to access the "To-Do" list in MyUNT. Check for "Verification" flags—failing to resolve these by August will cancel your initial disbursement.

  • ✓ Complete Eagle Scholarship Portal
  • ✓ Monitor "Task" Tile in MyUNT
  • ✓ Confirm "Census Date" on Academic Calendar
Continuing & Transfer

Renewal & Transfer Credit

Continuing students must re-apply for scholarships every year starting Sept 1st. Transfer students: ensure your final transcripts are received; otherwise, your TEXAS Grant or merit aid won't trigger for the next term.

  • ✓ Maintain 2.0+ GPA for SAP
  • ✓ Complete 30 hours per year (TEXAS Grant)
  • ✓ Update FAFSA annually in October

The TASFA Note

TASFA students must mail or hand-deliver documents. Digital submission is not currently available for state-only aid.

Contractual Acceptance

Aid is merely an offer until you sign the portal.

  • 1. Go to MyUNT > Financial Aid.
  • 2. Click "Accept/Decline" for 2024.
  • 3. Review Loan Interest Rates first.

The "SAP" Barrier

Satisfactory Progress2.0 GPA + 67% Completion Rate. is the law. Transfer students: your transfer GPA counts toward your first year SAP check at UNT.

Dropping past the Census Date counts as a "Failed Attempt" in the 67% ratio calculation.

! Advanced Literacy

Refunds are not "Gifts"

A refund is the surplus of your aid after tuition. If your aid includes loans, that "refund" check is accruing interest the moment it hits your bank account.

Parental Non-Filer Status

If parents didn't file taxes, don't leave the field blank. Submit the "Parent Non-Filer Statement" to the financial aid portal.
